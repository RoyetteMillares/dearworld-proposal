Technology Stack

Frontend: Next.js 16 | Backend: Firebase | Storage: AWS S3 | Security: Cloudflare Turnstile

Why Next.js 16 is Critical for Dear World

1. Lightning-Fast Image Loading: Native Image component optimizes every photo: responsive resizing, lazy loading, WebP, and blur placeholders. Photos load 3-5x faster, preserving narrative flow.

2. Server-Side Rendering (SEO): Ensures every story, photo, and creator profile is fully indexable by Google. A competitive advantage worth $50,000+ in marketing.

3. Built-In Performance: Handles code splitting and automatic optimization out-of-the-box. No need for specialized performance engineers.

4. Global Edge Caching with Vercel: Photos served from edge servers near the viewer (Tokyo, Berlin, São Paulo) arrive instantly.

5. Mobile-First by Default: Over 80% of consumption is on mobile. Interactions feel smooth even on 3G connections.

6. Future-Proof for Scale: Scales from hundreds to millions of stories without refactoring.

The Bottom Line: Dear World's audience won't experience slow image loads or janky mobile experiences. They'll get the emotional storytelling experience that builds deep audience connection.

Enterprise-Grade Security Architecture

1 Cloudflare Turnstile: Bot-resistant forms. No spam or fake accounts.
2 Firebase Auth: Industry-standard OAuth 2.0. User passwords never stored on servers.
3 OWASP Top 10: Protection against SQL Injection, XSS, and CSRF.
4 Cloudflare DDoS Protection: Malicious traffic filtered at the edge. Zero downtime.
5 Rate Limiting & WAF: Brute force attempts blocked after 5 failures.
6 End-to-End Encryption: TLS 1.3 in transit and AES-256 at rest.
